4. **Contract-first design.** `verdict/schema.py` + `CONTRACTS.md` as a single source of truth, with `.model_dump_json()` on every cross-module type. Pydantic v2 validation catches contract drift at import time.
5. **Reproducibility is real.** Re-running the command produces byte-identical numbers (modulo `created_at`) — no RNG, no clock dependence, no network. Verified twice.
6. **Cost model is crypto-correct.** PancakeSwap v2 0.25% LP fee + 30 bps slippage + optional gas is the right stack for BSC DEX execution; the Binance spot fallback (10 bps + 5 bps) is the right CEX floor.

## 5. What doesn't work / honest concerns

1. **Zero positive expected value on any of 12 candidate × asset pairs.** Even the *best* result (ETH momentum +0.87%) is below buy & hold (+12% benchmark median), below the Sharpe 1.0 gate, and inside the cost envelope. The engine produces no investable edge on the shipped fixtures.
2. **Mean-reversion archetype is structurally broken** (the team's own xfail). It can't profit on a clean synthetic oscillator, and on live data it lost 72–81%. That's 1 of 3 archetypes non-functional.
3. **Momentum's RSI floor is `> 50`** (`verdict/core/candidates.py:87`). Combined with the EMA stack this is fine, but it leaves RSI > 70 entries uncapped — in the walk-forward windows, momentum repeatedly entered near local tops (the −25% window 7 in 2024-Q1/Q2 is the smoking gun). The TP/SL ratio (8×ATR / 2×ATR = 4:1) is theoretically sound but rarely fills in 4h crypto volatility.
4. **Walk-forward `train` region is pure warmup, not re-fit** (`verdict/core/walkforward.py:14-16`). This is *more honest* than rolling re-fit but means specs are static — they can't adapt to regime change. The 2022 bear, 2023 chop, 2024 recovery, and 2025 bull each have different optimal parameters, and one fixed spec can't span all four.
5. **PancakeSwap slippage of 30 bps flat is optimistic for size.** Real BSC slippage on a $10k move through Pancake v2 is closer to 50–100 bps on secondary pairs (CAKE/USDT especially). The "honest" cost model is honest for retail size, optimistic for the protocol's actual deployable size.
6. **Sentiment is cosmetic at the decision layer.** The demo's `decision_matrix` has sentiment at 15% weight but `score=45.28` is identical across `conservative`/`balanced`/`aggressive` traits — the personality layer cannot bypass the risk gate, so traits only affect the narrative string, not the action. That's *correct* risk discipline, but it makes the "agent personality" pitch ring hollow.
7. **Track 1 (autonomous BSC executor) is empty.** `verdict/execution/__init__.py` is empty; the README is upfront about this. So this is a Strategy Skill (Track 2) submission only, not an end-to-end trading agent.

The 85% coverage, the xfail-honesty, and the byte-identical reproducibility are real differentiators.

The engine's whole point is to honestly say `NO_TRADE` when nothing works, and that's exactly what it does — but a judge who only looks at P&L will see a system that produces nothing tradable. The one xfail (mean-rev broken) and the consistently negative Sharpe ratios suggest the candidate generator needs a v2: regime-gated confluence (the team's own roadmap note), adaptive parameters per window, or a different archetype set entirely.

**Hackathon fit: strong on Track 2, weak on Track 1.** As a "Strategy Skill" submission that ships an honest `NO_TRADE` with full audit trail, this is credible and original. As an "AI Trading Agent" it does not trade — and the README is honest about that.

**Bottom line.** This is a *trustworthy* trading engine with no current edge, not a *profitable* trading engine. For a hackathon whose judging criteria reward "real technical execution, originality, and credibility," the credibility half is genuinely delivered. The edge half is not — and the team's own xfail and the walk-forward numbers show they know it. Whether that's a feature (honesty as moat) or a bug (no deliverable strategy) depends entirely on which rubric axis the judges weight more.
